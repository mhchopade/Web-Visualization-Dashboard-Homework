Latitude vs. Wind Speed Summary



Visualizations



As was predicted, there was no enlightening pattern in wind speed (MPH) with respect to latitude. However, investigation on meterological data suggests hemispheric prevailing winds occur between -60 to -30 and 30 to 60 latitudes, these are known as "westerlies" and is where dense cold air from the poles collides with light warm air from the equator creating a low pressure environment, apt for higher winds. Futher geographical and biome differences in northern hemisphere (e.g., greater landmass, warmer oceans) illustrate this point with greater frequency of tornadoes and hurricanes.
